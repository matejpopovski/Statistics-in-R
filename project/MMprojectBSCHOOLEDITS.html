INTRODUCTION & BACKGROUND

The game of basketball has changed over the years; in fact, the three-point line was not introduced to the NBA until 1961. Since then, players like Steph Curry have transformed the NBA once they played outside the three-point line and extended the court. Over the years, in the NBA, since the introduction and the subsequent revolution of the mid-2010s, the number of attempted 3-point shots (shots taken) and 3-point efficiency (average percentage of threes made) has steadily increased (Tomasz, 2023). Therefore, our project aims to answer whether NBA trends have descended into college basketball play by analyzing the March Madness teams 3-point attempts and makes between 2010 and 2023. To do so, we graphed the sum of attempts for all tournament teams by year in a graph and the sum of 3-point percentages for all tournament teams by year; additionally, we conducted a hypothesis test using the difference of means on both attempts and percentages made. Splitting the data from 2010 to 2015, 2015 was the first year the Warriors won a championship, and the 3-point trend started to take hold compared to the recent year (2016-2024).

THESIS

Based on our difference in means test on both 3-point shots attempted and 3-point shots made in the years before 2015 (2010-2015) and after 2016-present, we found evidence to suggest a difference in attempted 3-point shots between the samples (2016-present indicating a higher percentage of shot attempts) but failed to find strong evidence to suggest a significant difference in 3-point shots made across the two samples.

ASSUMPTIONS

Although two-sample t-tests operate under multiple assumptions, the primary (most relevant) assumption in this test isindependence; theresno connection between the samples; a value in one group does not relate to a value in the other sample.(Jones, 2024) both within each sample and across the two samples. Our data satisfies this requirement because 3-point attempts and makes of one team do not affect the attempts or makes of another team, both within each years tournament and across different years.

This graph displays the sum of three points taken by all tournament teams during March Madness. As you can see, there was a dramatic increase in attempted three-pointers between 2015 and 2019. However, since 2020, three-point attempts have started to decrease but are still significantly higher than in the years preceding 2015.

Unlike the NBA, where both shot attempts and percentage made have increased, tournament teams percentages during March Madness have largely decreased since 2015.

DISCUSSION

Based on our two graphs and two-variable difference of means t-tests, evidence suggests that 3-point shot attempts have increased, and the 3-point percentage has decreased over time. However, we lack evidence to conclude that NBA trends transcend into college basketball. Therefore, NBA trends may be trickling down into college play to a degree, but based on the variables chosen, further data analysis would need to be conducted to conclude whether or not NBA trends have significantly influenced the three-point shot overall

One possible explanation for this is that college players are not as skilled as NBA players, and therefore, although players are taking more shots, they are not as successful, explaining the decrease in the percentage. Other possible explanations are that our data is skewed because the research on NBA trends included averages across an entire season, whereas ours only comprised March Madness; therefore, other conclusions are possible if we included data for all teams across the entire college season. However, there would still be disparity as the league comprises 30 teams, whereas Division One basketball comprises 358 teams, possibly exasperating the skill distribution.

Therefore, a more in-depth statistical analysis could isolate and compare the stats of the NBA draft picks. Isolating the highest-caliber players could better indicate NBA to college trends, as college basketball players have a broader variation of skill and ability compared to pros. Another way to approach it would be to isolate more skilled teams year by year or take entire season averages on the D1 level instead of only tournament play. In sum, there could be many directions to take in determining how professional play influences; however, due to an evidently larger pool (college vs.pro) and overall, there are many limitations to determining correlating trends. Still, our project is a starting point for further analysis as it provides some suggesting evidence of a potential correlation between NBA and college play.
